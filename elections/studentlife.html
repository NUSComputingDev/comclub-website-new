Student Life

Student Life is the largest and most active Wing in Comp Club, where we plan and organise events that shape the Computing experience - from Freshmen's first impression of SoC to the Alumni's point of contact post-graduation.

Our events include Freshmen Orientation Projects and Student Engagement. Beyond events and activities, we take care of the shared Student Lounge and strive to promote active communication between Comp Club and the student body.

Ultimately, we aspire to bring the SoC community together and help forge everlasting friendships.

Introduction

Vice President of Student Life [VP(SL)] oversees all student life-related activities under FOP Cell and Students Engagement Cell and provides a vision that all the events aim towards.

Roles

VP(SL) works with the directors and relevant cells within Computing Club to:

  • Ensure the planning and operations of student life-related events go smoothly.
  • Provide administrative and operational instructions to the directors, project directors and their teams.
  • Strike a balance between the success of these event planning and the well-being of all personnel involved.
  • Meet the expectations of all the stakeholders, including the advisors, the club, the committee and the participants to guarantee effective and meaningful experiences for everyone.

Experience

For me, the experience was exciting and meaningful. As the leader of the largest sub-committee in the club, I was engaged in Com Club matters consistently throughout the year, engaging with different OComms from recruitment, and proposal planning all the way to execution and post-event reflection.

I also focused on ensuring my wing are bonded and helpful with each other throughout the year. This was achieved by our multiple gatherings and outings beyond our meeting time! This allows us to be closer with each other, which is crucial during our busy periods.

At each of the events, I always attended alongside the project directors to help oversee the operations of the events, while helping out with administrative matters or emergency situations. For example, I was staying with the OComm for FOW throughout 4 days of the camp to ensure the team were fine and not overwhelmed by the scale of this physical camp.

Being an international student, I also guided my wing to focus on inclusivity and fairness to all students in SoC, regardless of race, gender or nationality. This has allowed us to have diversity and representation in our events and allowing SoC students to form new friendships with others through our events like COMaraderie and Meliorium.

Overall, I am grateful to have completed my responsibility as an VP(SL) and I have definitely grown a lot with my leadership and management skills. To the potential candidates: It is going to be a tough and tiring journey to lead and serve - but the joy and satisfaction will come when you know the impact our events and our camps have had on every student experience in SoC.

Introduction

As part of the FOP cell, we organise 5 events! FSC, EFOP, FOW, RAG & BASH. Every year this lineup of events will be decided by the FOP cell, if you are looking to organise additional events feel free to come up with a proposal for approval!

Roles

The FOP cell is responsible for the recruitment of PDs for the respective events and liaison with Computing Club and the advisors.

Apart from the management committee and advisors, we work closely with many students, recruiting close to 300 student OGLs and committee members, forming the largest cell in Computing Club. Hence, you will get to meet friends from every major.

Workload wise, the FOP cell gets the busiest during our summer break closer to when the incoming batch of freshies come in. From testing games to packing logistics, summer will definitely be fun filled for the FOP cell :) However, this doesn't mean that your semesters are untouched too, although more chill, there will still be periods where planning early is essential.

We have definitely learnt a lot during our year in Computing Club, working with new people is definitely not an easy task, but with an open mind and willingness to try, it will definitely be a fruitful experience in FOP cell!

Events

Computing Freshmen Social Camp (FSC)

FSC marks the start of SOC's very first FOP Event. Freshmen get to immerse themselves in exhilarating outdoor games and sports, while building friendships that lasts beyond the end of the camp. This camp also serves to introduce and expose the freshmen to other FOP events such as Rag & Flag and Bash.

Freshmen Orientation Week (FOW)

A 4-day physical camp where we welcome freshmen into the SoC community and see to the building of new friendships that would follow them through their time at the University and beyond. FOW encourages social interactions between freshmen, as well as with seniors currently in SoC.

BASH

A pageant where 5 pairs of BASHies culminate into one finale night full of fun, laughter, and eyecandy.

E-Freshmen Orientation Program (E-FOP)

A 2-day online camp with the main goal of integrating the international freshmen into NUS SoC. Freshies will get the opportunity to make new friends through engaging in exciting activities facilitated online.

Introduction

The Student Engagement cell focuses on creating avenues for students to forge new and long lasting connections within SoC. From Computing Day to COMaraderie, we organise a myriad of events to cater to the entire student body. Join the SR cell to look forward to enriching student life here in SoC.

Experience

Working with people from diverse backgrounds has given us new perspectives in the way we problem solve and plan ahead for certain elements in our events! The most fulfilling part would have to be seeing our participants enjoy themselves while engaging in our event activities.

Events

As a cell that serves both supporting and creative roles, we collaborate with all cells in the club closely to help them with their events' publicity materials. Also, we initiate our own events such as social media challenges and video series.

COMaraderie

Pairing participants up, we challenged pairs to embark on a quest to conquer as many challenges as they can, in the hopes of emerging the battle as the top performers to claim attractive prizes.

Meliorium

A one-day cultural event which aims in assimilating international students to the culture here in Singapore. As participants from different nationalities have fun bond together through the activities, we celebrate the cultural diversity here in SoC and promote a vibrant student life here in SoC.

HomeCOMing

Helf in conjunction with the opening of our long awaited COM3, HomeCOMing is back bigger than ever. From carnival games to project showcases by our talented computing students, Homecoming celebrates SoC's presence in the technology sector.
